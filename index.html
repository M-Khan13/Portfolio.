How the Workflow Moves Forward

S1

It always start by diving deep into the core idea — asking questions, exploring possibilities, and figuring out what really needs to be built. Whether it's an app, a feature, or just a concept, I like to lay everything out first and understand the ‘why’ behind it.

S2

Once I have a direction, I draw inspiration from everywhere — UI galleries, design communities, everyday objects, and even architecture. I build mood boards and visual references that set the tone for what I’m about to create.

S3

This is where ideas start to take shape. I jump into Figma and begin wireframing or prototyping, depending on the project. It’s less about perfection here and more about testing flows, layouts, and functionality. I treat this as a sketchbook — free to explore and iterate.

S4

After the structure feels right, I start tightening up the visuals — playing with typography, refining spacing, adding hierarchy, and making sure everything feels balanced. I pay close attention to details that elevate the overall experience, while keeping things clean and user-friendly.

S5

Once the design feels solid, I package it all up — clean files, assets, and organized components, ready to hand off. I also like to stick around to answer questions, tweak based on feedback, and make sure the vision stays intact till the end.
